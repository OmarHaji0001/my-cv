AutoMentor is well-designed, simulation-based educational package that gives learners an almost real, interactive driving experience. The small city environment is programmed using the Unity game engine, populated with user-controlled car, AI-driven cars and non-player characters. It contains a particular set of rules and scenarios to test and improve userâ€™s driving skills. While playing, the player will drive in the city in their car using a physical driving kit. Just as in actual physical driving controller in a real car, when everything is realistic, the game is noticed to have any violation of any rule or a mistake made by the player and, in turn, penalizes it. There is notably a system called "rewind," in which the players may go back to the moments that led to a mistake, where they can correct it. The AI cars follow realistic driving patterns, creating a learning environment that mirrors real-world road conditions. This project will already bridge the enormous gap between theoretical knowledge regarding driving and practical application, with a very safe and controlled learning environment where learners may practice and perfect their driving skills.

When the user enters the stop zone game object, he should at least fully stop for 5 seconds to ensure that no risks threaten his safety and use indicators to show his intended destination. Any breaking of these rules will get him penalized. When the AI car sensor detects the stop sign game object, the AI script will use equations of motion by getting the velocity of the AI car and the remaining distance between the first detection position and the stop zone position to apply the equations, to ensure that the AI car will stop exactly at the correct stop zone. After waiting for 5 seconds, the AI car will decide to move and turn the wanted indicator on, but before moving, the AI car will check if there are cars on the left if it wants to go in the right direction, and it'll check right and left if it wants to go the left direction.
